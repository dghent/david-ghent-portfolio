Basic Blading Tricks

A guide to most of the basic tricks you could learn as a new blader. A little side project with all the time I had sheltering in place during the pandemic of 2020.

#rollerbladingisback

Frontside

Probably the best beginner trick to start with. Approach with speed and jump with both feet and land equally balanced on the center of your frames, with your legs shoulder width apart.

Backside

Similar to a frontside, but slightly more difficult with the rail to your back. You'll find it'll feel like you have to turn a bit more than a frontside.

Royale

Much like a frontside, but lean both your ankles backward. You'll find it's much easier to bend with your knees to allow your ankles to rest. Make sure you lean forward otherwise you'll slip back.

Backside Royale

Very similar to a frontside royale but a bit more challenging since the rail is behind you.

Frontside Full Torque

Once you've mastered a frontside try leaning your into your groove on your front foot too. It looks simple but can be challenging to keep your front foot leaning forward. Bend your knees and space out your legs to make it easier to lean your ankles.

Backside Full Torque

Similar to a frontside full torque, but backside. Some find this much easier than the frontside version, some vice versa. Start with the one that's most comfortable to you and then work on the other.

Soul Grind

A classic trick - easy to learn but hard to jump on with both feet at first. Practise by stepping on with your soul foot first. Once mastered it's one of the easier tricks to balance for longer grinds.

Acid Soul Grind

Similar to a soul grind, but your front foot is angeled open. Rely on your soul foot to be solid before trying this one.

Mizou

This is another good starter soul trick. Start by learning to put weight on your soul foot and getting comfortable with your back foot on the h-block. Balance by leaning your soul foot on top of the rail and keeping a wide stance.

Makio

When you're mastered the basic soul tricks try this classic one footed trick. The key to holding the grab is learning over on your soul foot while bending your knees and keeping holding your foot up.

Pornstar

Silly name, but a fun trick. After mastering the Makio this trick should come fairly easy. It's easiest if most of your weight is on your soul foot.

Mistrial

The stylish version of the mizou, this trick looks great if you bend your knees to keep the back foot nice and angeled. Some prefer to do this with the other soul foot to take advantage of a stronger backside backslide foot.

X-Grind

Keep both soul feet inline and stay completely on top of the rail. Some prefer to have their natural soul foot in front, some prefer behind.

Backslide

Another classic trick, but more advanced. Once you're comfortabe with royales try lifting your front foot and grabbing it. To learn this trick try using more speed to help you balance.
